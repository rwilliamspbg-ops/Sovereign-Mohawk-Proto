# Naming and Attribution

The "Sovereign Mohawk Protocol" name draws inspiration from sovereignty, resilience, and decentralized governance goals in the technical architecture.

This project does not claim, appropriate, or represent the cultural, intellectual, or traditional knowledge/property of the Kanien'keha:ka (Mohawk) people or any Indigenous nation.

Within this codebase, MOHAWK is also used as a technical shorthand:

- Mobile Offloading Heterogeneous Adaptive Weights for Knowledge

The maintainers acknowledge and respect Indigenous sovereignty and self-determination, including the Kanien'keha:ka as Keepers of the Eastern Door in the Haudenosaunee Confederacy.

If naming concerns arise, please open an issue for discussion and potential updates.
